Criminal Convictions:

You do not have to tell us about any convictions that are spent (as defined by the Rehabilitation of Offenders Act 1974), or about any cautions, reprimands or final warnings.

If you declare that you have an unspent conviction the provider may contact you for further information. Declaring a conviction will not necessarily bar you from being offered a place with the provider.

Please note that some providers, such as care, childcare or health studies, require you to undergo a Criminal Records Bureau disclosure. This will include both spent and unspent convictions, as well as cautions, reprimands and final warnings.

For further advice about whether convictions are unspent please contact the NACRO Helpline on 020 7840 7200.
